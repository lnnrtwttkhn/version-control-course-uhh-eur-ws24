The issue of computational reproducibility in science

‚Äú‚Ä¶ when the same analysis steps performed on the same dataset consistently produce the same answer.‚Äù 1

The problem

  • about more than half of research is not reproducible 2
    • research data, code, software & materials are often not available ‚Äúupon reasonable [sic] request‚Äù
    • if resources are shared, they are often incomplete
  • 90% of researchers: ‚Äúreproducibility crisis‚Äù (N = 1576) 3

Why?

  • computational reproducibility is hard
  • researchers lack training
  • incentives are not (yet) aligned 4
  • ‚Äúnatural selection of bad science‚Äù 5

‚Äú‚Ä¶ accumulated evidence indicates [‚Ä¶] substantial room for improvement with regard to research practices to maximize the efficiency of the research community‚Äôs use of the public‚Äôs financial investment.‚Äù (Munaf√≤ et al., 2017)

We need a professional toolkit for digital research!

  • also called ‚Äúanalytical reproducibility‚Äù
  • in contrast: ‚ÄúReplication‚Äù = same analysis on different data
  • computational reproducibility should be a minimum requirement
  • 90% of respondents to a survey in Nature agreed that there is a ‚Äúreproducibility crisis‚Äù
  • non-scientist friends react very surprised at the lack of professionalization in science
  • we are currently violating the public‚Äôs trust = deep ethical problem
  • natural selection: as a researcher, what‚Äôs good for your career is not necessarily good for science (how to get funding, how to get published, how to get cited)
  • journals are interested in selling us back our data, not improving the quality of science

What is version control?

‚ÄúVersion control is a systematic approach to record changes made in a [‚Ä¶] set of files, over time. This allows you and your collaborators to track the history, see what changed, and recall specific versions later [‚Ä¶]‚Äù (Turing Way)

keep track of changes in a directory (a ‚Äúrepository‚Äù)

take snapshots (‚Äúcommits‚Äù) of your repo at any time

know the history: what was changed when by whom

compare commits and go back to any previous state

work on parallel ‚Äúbranches‚Äù & flexibly ‚Äúmerge‚Äù them

‚Äúpush‚Äù your repo to a ‚Äúremote‚Äù location & share it

share repos on platforms like GitHub or GitLab

work together on the same files at the same time

others can read, copy, edit and suggest changes

make your repo public and openly share your work

What is Git?

  • most popular version control system
  • free, open-source command-line tool
  • graphical user interfaces exist, e.g., GitKraken
  • standard tool for most (all?) software developers
  • 100 million GitHub users 6
  • Version control is relevant for anyone who wants to track the evolution of digital objects
